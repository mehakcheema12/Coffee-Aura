Coffee Science

Understanding the chemistry behind your favorite brew

Maillard Reaction

The Maillard reaction occurs when coffee beans are roasted, causing amino acids and reducing sugars to react. This complex series of reactions creates hundreds of flavor and aroma compounds that give coffee its distinctive taste.

Aroma Compounds

Over 800 volatile compounds contribute to coffee's aroma. Key compounds include:

  • Furans: Caramel-like sweetness
  • Pyrazines: Earthy, nutty aromas
  • Aldehydes: Fruity, floral notes

Caffeine Knowledge

Understanding the world's most popular psychoactive substance

What is Caffeine?

Caffeine is a natural stimulant most commonly found in coffee, tea, and cacao plants. It works by stimulating the brain and central nervous system, helping you stay alert and preventing the onset of tiredness.

How It Works

Caffeine's primary mechanism is blocking adenosine receptors in the brain. Adenosine is a neurotransmitter that promotes sleep and relaxation. By blocking these receptors, caffeine increases neural activity.

Caffeine Facts

Half-Life

Caffeine's half-life is about 5 hours, meaning it takes 5 hours for your body to eliminate half of the caffeine you consumed.

World Consumption

Approximately 2.25 billion cups of coffee are consumed worldwide every day.

Coffee Journey

From bean to cup - the fascinating journey of coffee

Planting

Coffee starts as a seed planted in nurseries. After 6-12 months, the seedlings are transplanted to coffee farms.

Harvesting

Coffee cherries are hand-picked when ripe, typically 3-4 years after planting. Selective picking ensures only ripe cherries are harvested.

Processing

The beans are extracted from the cherries using either dry (natural) or wet (washed) methods, then dried to about 11% moisture content.

Milling

The dried beans are hulled, polished, graded, and sorted by size and weight before being exported.

Roasting

Green coffee beans are roasted at 200-250°C, developing flavor, aroma, and color through chemical changes.

Grinding

Roasted beans are ground to different consistencies based on brewing method - from fine (espresso) to coarse (French press).

Brewing

Hot water extracts flavors from ground coffee, with methods ranging from espresso machines to pour-over drippers.

Pour Over Method

About This Method

The pour over method involves manually pouring hot water over coffee grounds in a filter. The water drains through the coffee and filter into a carafe or mug. This method allows for precise control over water temperature, pour rate, and brew time, resulting in a clean, nuanced cup that великолепный highlights the coffee's origin characteristics.

Brew Ratio

1:16 coffee to water (e.g., 20g coffee to 320g water)

Brewing Steps

1

Boil water and let it cool slightly. Rinse paper filter with hot water to remove paper taste and preheat brewer.

2

Add medium-fine ground coffee to filter. Gently shake to level bed.

3

Start timer and pour enough water to saturate all grounds. Let bloom for 30 seconds.
